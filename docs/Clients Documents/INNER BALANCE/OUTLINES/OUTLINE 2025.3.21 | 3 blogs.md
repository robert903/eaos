[Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT)](https://www.innerbalance.com/p/hormone-replacement-therapy-how-it-works/) is a medical treatment that restores optimal levels of sex hormones—primarily estrogen, progesterone, and sometimes testosterone—in women [experiencing symptoms](https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng23) related to hormone decline. HRT is [not just for menopausal women](https://www.innerbalance.com/p/ask-doctor-sarah-can-you-start-hrt-before-menopause/). Hormonal decline often begins in the early to mid-30s and can affect women of all ages. At Inner Balance, we view HRT as a proactive health strategy, not a last resort.

## **What Does Bioidentical Hormone Therapy Cost?**

Costs vary depending on the type of hormone, the delivery method, and whether you’re using compounded or traditional options. Typically they are cheaper out of pocket due to the tiered approach that insurance uses.

* **Compounded personalized therapies with progesterone**: $100-$400/month
* **Traditional patches, pills and low dose vaginal creams**: May be covered by insurance; out-of-pocket costs $30–$100/month
* **Pellets**: $300–$500/month and placed every 3 months (often not covered by insurance)

Consultation and lab work may be an additional cost depending on your provider.

## **Is Bioidentical HRT Safe?**

This is the question I get asked most—and it’s a valid one. The short answer [YES!](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19179815/) They are also the preferred method of HRT based on the [science.](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19179815/)

### **What the Research Shows:**

* Data shows that bioidentical hormones are associated with [lower risks](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19179815/), including the risk of breast cancer and cardiovascular disease, [and are more efficacious](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19179815/) than their synthetic and animal-derived counterparts. Until evidence is found to the contrary, bioidentical hormones [remain the preferred method of HRT](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19179815/).
* **FDA-approved bioidentical hormones (like estradiol and micronized progesterone)** are considered safe and effective when prescribed and monitored correctly.
* **Compounded combinations use FDA-approved bioidentical hormones** but are not manufactured and therefore the finished product is not FDA-approved.
* Working with a compounding pharmacy that is certified, licensed in the united states is important.

**Breast Cancer and BHRT:**

* The history and misinformation between HRT and breast cancer risk is complex. MOST studies have shown **no increased risk with bioidentical estradiol and progesterone**, and even a [***lower risk***](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6386596/#:~:text=After%2018%20years%20of%20cumulative,%2DCEE%20trial%20(7).) compared to synthetic progestins. [With the largest study (randomized control trial) showing a protective effect.](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2653735)
* Anyone with a family history of hormone-sensitive cancers is eligible. This is NOT a reason not to get started. If you have a personal history, your quality of life matters too, and a risk benefit conversation with a qualified provider can help you make you make your decision.

**The bottom line?** BHRT is safe for the vast majority of women when prescribed by a trained hormone specialist and proper followup.
