## Guidelines

### Content Structure & Headings

* Remove all formatting that is provided by ChatGPT (including fonts)
* Use a **hierarchical heading structure** for clarity and SEO optimization.
  + **H2s** should be used for primary sections.
  + **H3s** should be used for subsections within an H2.
  + **H4s** may be used for further breakdowns where necessary.
* **Incorporate target keywords** naturally in H2 headings where appropriate.

### Formatting & Links

* **Embed links** directly in the text using the hyperlink function (Cmd + K in Google Docs) instead of adding footnotes. This makes it easier to paste into the blog.
* **Internal linking priority**:
  + Whenever possible, link to **relevant InnerBalance articles** instead of external sources.
  + Only link externally when absolutely necessary (e.g., scientific studies, government resources).

### Content Organization & Flow

* Ensure each article delivers on what the **title promises** as quickly as possible.
  + The **core content (the "meat" of the article)** should appear within the **first 10-20%** of the article.
  + Any peripheral or supporting information should be moved to the **bottom third** of the article (e.g., FAQs, additional resources).
* Consider restructuring longer articles where necessary:
  + Lists should be **presented early** (e.g., in "12 Signs" articles, present the list first).
  + Supporting details, explanations, and expanded points can follow in a structured manner.

### Images & Graphics

* **Include at least one relevant image** in each article before publishing.
* Borrowing **graphics from cited sources** is fine, but **original graphics** are ideal.
* Use **high-quality, engaging visuals** to break up text and improve readability.

### Call-to-Action (CTA) Components

* Each article should include **at least one CTA**, such as:
  + Graphics with a call-to-action.
  + Text-based CTAs within content.
  + Buttons or embedded email forms.
* CTAs should be **strategically placed** where engagement is highest (e.g., after a key insight or at the end of an article).
