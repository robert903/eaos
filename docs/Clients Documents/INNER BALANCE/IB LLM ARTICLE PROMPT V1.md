# Business Context

The outlines you produce will be made into articles on the Inner Balance website. \*\*The strategy\*\* behind this content on that website is to guide prospective customers through a well-defined journey starting from symptom awareness and general information, moving through detailed education about hormone replacement therapy (HRT), and ultimately leading them toward consideration and/or purchase of Inner Balance HRT products or services.

## Stages of the User Journey

1. \*\*Acute Symptoms & Initial Concerns\*\*

- \*\*User Mindset:\*\* Experiencing unexplained issues like fatigue, hair loss, or other stress-related symptoms.

- \*\*Content Purpose:\*\* Validate their symptoms, introduce the idea that hormonal imbalance could be the root cause, and prompt them to explore further.

- \*\*Content Types:\*\* Symptom-specific articles, checklists, quizzes (e.g., "How to know if my hormones are imbalanced").

2. \*\*Hormonal Awareness & Menopause Education\*\*

- \*\*User Mindset:\*\* Beginning to connect symptoms with menopause, perimenopause, or hormonal changes.

- \*\*Content Purpose:\*\* Educate about the role of hormones, explain signs of imbalance, and position menopause as a possible explanation.

- \*\*Content Types:\*\* Articles on menopause symptoms, low estrogen, high cortisol, PCOS, endometriosis, and early menopause.

3. \*\*Introduction to HRT as a Solution\*\*

- \*\*User Mindset:\*\* Aware of HRT, curious about how it works, evaluating its relevance to their condition.

- \*\*Content Purpose:\*\* Provide foundational knowledge on HRT, explain types (pills, gels, creams), introduce bioidentical vs synthetic options, and explore alternatives.

- \*\*Content Types:\*\* "What is HRT?", treatment comparisons, HRT alternatives, types of delivery methods.

4. \*\*Evaluating Effects & Side Effects\*\*

- \*\*User Mindset:\*\* Actively researching benefits, risks, and real-life outcomes of HRT.

- \*\*Content Purpose:\*\* Address benefits and potential side effects, offer before/after insights, and link symptoms to HRT outcomes.

- \*\*Content Types:\*\* Benefits of HRT, pros/cons, side effects, before/after experiences, symptom-specific effects (e.g., HRT for osteoporosis or weight gain).

5. \*\*Commercial Intent & Action\*\*

- \*\*User Mindset:\*\* Ready to take action, looking for practical next steps.

- \*\*Content Purpose:\*\* Guide users on how to start HRT, provide cost and insurance details, and highlight why Inner Balance is a trusted provider.

- \*\*Content Types:\*\* "How to start HRT," cost and insurance guides, where to buy HRT, Inner Balance product/service pages.

## Content's Role in Driving Purchase

- Each content piece not only informs but \*\*guides the user deeper\*\* into their journey—helping them move from symptom discovery to treatment consideration.

- The structure ensures:

- \*\*Symptom-related content\*\* leads naturally to hormone education.

- \*\*Hormone awareness content\*\* bridges into treatment options.

- \*\*HRT-focused content\*\* prepares users to evaluate and act.

- \*\*Commercial content\*\* removes friction and encourages conversion with Inner Balance.

- Strategic internal links and clear calls-to-action ensure users continue progressing toward a decision, staying within Inner Balance's ecosystem.

## Strategy

A well-structured outline strategy section should include the following sections:

\*\*(H3) Where it fits in:\*\*

- Explain the article's position in your content strategy

- Identify the stage in the user journey this content serves

\*\*Examples:\*\*

- "This article is the center of the Inner Balance informational content universe. Every time we refer to hormone replacement therapy in another article, it will point back to this one."

- "This article serves as a bridge between informational queries about conditions and symptoms and HRT-centric content."

- "This is a top of funnel article to transition readers toward more informational materials and ultimately closer to commercial content."

\*\*(H3) Goal:\*\*

- List 1-4 specific objectives for the article

- Focus on what you want readers to know, feel, or do after reading

- Include both content goals and business goals

\*\*Examples:\*\*

- "Establish trust and authority in the publication"

- "Position Inner Balance's solution favorably relative to alternatives (in the treatments section)"

- "Provide clear, accessible information on when a woman might consider HRT"

- "Create a link between menopause symptoms and HRT benefits"

- "Inspire women to convince themselves that HRT is a legitimate solution"

- "Counter objections and suggest that it works to resolve symptoms"

- "Provide a comprehensive roadmap for starting HRT"

### Tone:

- Define the voice and style of the article

- Use 2-3 adjectives to describe the tone (e.g., "Educational and unassuming")

- Ensure tone aligns with your brand voice

### Differentiation:

- Explain how this article differs from competition in search engines

- Highlight unique angles or approaches

- Emphasize what makes your content more valuable

### Word count:

- Specify the target length (e.g., "1000-1500 words")

- Adjust based on topic complexity and depth needed
