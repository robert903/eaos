# Inner Balance GSC Summary Report

An analysis of the /p/learn/ URLs reveals several critical technical SEO issues consistently hindering content performance. The most prominent are missing headers, visible HTML on pages, and overly long anchor links.

### Pervasive "Missing Headers":

* + Missing headers within a number of articles have been identified as the primary cause of:
    - Minimal/subdued/poor performance
    - Hindered SEO and user experience (UX)
    - Low Click-Through Rates (CTR)
    - Limited SEO potential

| Missing Headers | URLs | Impressions (90 DAYS) | Clicks (90 DAYS) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| FALSE | 10 | 11,318 | 27 |
| TRUE | 25 | 11,113 | 109 |

### Frequent "Visible HTML" Issue:

* + A large number of articles contain visible html on the page
  + This is a critical issue that contributes to reduced performance, zero clicks or low CTR.

### Outdated Indexed Content is a Major Recurring Problem:

* + Many URLs have a Last Crawl Date that is earlier than their Modified Date.
  + This means any recent fixes or content improvements are not being seen or evaluated by Google, directly impacting performance. It's a bottleneck even for pages with fewer other technical issues.

### Even "Technically Cleaner" Pages Suffer:

* + Pages where there are no missing headers, no visible HTML within the page and without overly long anchor texts (e.g., .../[ask-dr-sarah-can-you-start-menopause-in-your-30s](https://www.innerbalance.com/p/learn/ask-dr-sarah-can-you-start-menopause-in-your-30s-or-even-20s/).../, .../[can-hrt-stop-hair-loss](https://www.innerbalance.com/p/learn/can-hrt-stop-hair-loss-understanding-the-connection-and-your-options-inner-balance/).../) still often show poor CTR or low visibility.
  + For these, the analysis points towards:
    - Low search volume/high competition/content misalignment.
    - Poor SERP snippet appeal.

### Key Takeaways for /p/learn/ URLs:

* Fix Fundamental On-Page SEO: Address "Missing Headers" (ensure proper H2-Hn structure) and resolve "Visible HTML" issues site-wide for this section.
* Prioritize Fresh Indexing: Develop a strategy to ensure Google crawls and indexes the latest modified versions of these pages. This might involve submitting sitemaps more frequently, using the "Request Indexing" tool in GSC for key updated pages, and improving internal linking.
* Optimize for CTR: Once the technical and indexing issues are addressed, focus heavily on improving SERP snippet appeal (titles, meta descriptions) to convert impressions into clicks.
* Resolve Crawl Blocks: Investigate why some pages are not being crawled/indexed at all.

The data strongly suggests that the blog content has significant potential (indicated by impressions) that is being severely hampered by foundational technical SEO problems and issues with Google seeing the most current, improved versions of the content.
