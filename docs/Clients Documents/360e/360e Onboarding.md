### **1. WordPress Website Access**

**What this is for:** Your WordPress dashboard is the control center for your website. Granting us administrator access allows us to update content, install necessary tools (like tracking codes), improve security, and ensure everything runs smoothly.

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Log In to WordPress:** Open your web browser and go to your website's login page, which is usually my360e.com/wp-admin. Log in with your username and password.
2. **Go to "Users":** Once logged in, look at the menu on the left-hand side of the screen. Hover your mouse over "Users" and a sub-menu will appear. Click on **"Add New"**.
3. **Fill Out the "Add New User" Form:** You will see a form with a few fields.
   * **Username (required):** Create a simple username for the first team member. We suggest simply **trevor** if available or **elegantatomics\_trevor**.

   * **Password:** WordPress will automatically generate a strong password. You can leave this as is.
   * **Send User Notification:** Make sure the box next to "Send the new user an email about their account" is checked. This is important as it sends us the link to set up our password.
   * **Role:** This is the most important step. Click the dropdown menu next to "Role" and select **"Administrator"**.
4. **Add the User:** Click the blue **"Add New User"** button at the bottom of the page.
5. **Repeat for the Second User:** Follow steps 2-4 again to create a second account for Robert.

### **2. Setting Up and Granting Access to Google Search Console**

**What this is for:** Google Search Console is a free tool from Google that helps you understand how your website performs in Google search results. It shows you which keywords people use to find you, alerts you to website errors, and confirms that Google can find and crawl your site.

Since this may be a new setup, we will walk through creating the account first. **If you already have an account, skip to #4.**

**Step-by-Step Instructions:**

1. **Go to Google Search Console:** Click this link to go to the official page: <https://search.google.com/search-console> . You will need to sign in with your primary Google account.
2. **Add Your Website:**
   * You will see two options: "Domain" and "URL prefix". For simplicity, please choose the **"URL prefix"** option on the right.
   * In the box, type your full website address exactly as it appears in the browser bar (e.g., https://my360e.com).
   * Click **"CONTINUE"**.
3. **Verify Website Ownership:** Google needs to confirm you own the website. It will present several ways to do this. The easiest method is usually the **"HTML tag"** option.
   * Click on **"HTML tag"** to expand it.
   * You will see a small piece of code that looks like <meta name="google-site-verification" ... />.
   * Click the **"COPY"** button next to this code.
   * **Do not click "VERIFY" yet!**
   * We can handle the rest of this verification step for you once we have WordPress access. Simply save this copied HTML tag in a notepad file for now. Once we log in to WordPress, we can easily add it to the website and complete the verification.
4. **Add Us as Users (After Verification):** Once the site is verified, you can add us as users.
   * From the main Search Console dashboard, click on **"Settings"** near the bottom of the left-hand menu.
   * Click on **"Users and permissions"**.
   * Click the blue **"ADD USER"** button in the top right corner.
   * In the "Email address" field, enter trevor@elegantatomics.com, robert@elegantatomics.com.
   * For "Permission", make sure **"Full"** is selected from the dropdown. This gives us the ability to submit sitemaps and fix any issues that arise.
   * Click **"ADD"**.

### **Recommendation: Implement Microsoft Clarity**

**What this is and why it's valuable:**We strongly recommend setting up Microsoft Clarity. It’s a free tool that helps us see how real people are using your website. It provides:

* **Heatmaps:** Visual guides that show where users click, move their mouse, and scroll most often. This helps us see what parts of your pages are getting the most attention.
* **Session Recordings:** Anonymous recordings of individual visitor sessions. We can watch a playback of a user's journey on the site to identify points of confusion or frustration.

Using Clarity is like looking over a user's shoulder to understand their experience, which provides invaluable insights for improving the website's design and functionality.

**How to get started:**

1. **Create an Account:** Go to [clarity.microsoft.com](http://clarity.microsoft.com) and sign up for a free account.
2. **Add a New Project:** Once you're signed in, you'll be prompted to add a "new project". Enter your website's name and URL.
3. **Get the Tracking Code:** Clarity will provide you with a small piece of tracking code.
   * **You do not need to do anything else!**
   * Simply **copy this code** and send it to us in an email. Once we have WordPress access (from Step 1), we will handle the technical task of installing this code onto your website for you.
