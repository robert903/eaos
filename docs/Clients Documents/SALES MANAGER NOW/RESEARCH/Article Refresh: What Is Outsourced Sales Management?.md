## **Article Refresh: What Is Outsourced Sales Management?**

Areas for Improvement in the Outsourced Sales Management Article

**1. Limited Comparison of Hiring Fractional vs. Full-Time Sales Managers**

* **Problem**: The article compares fractional and full-time sales managers primarily on monetary cost (e.g., via a compensation chart) but overlooks other ease-of-hiring factors like time, flexibility, and risk.
* **Solution**: Expand the comparison to include non-monetary aspects such as speed of onboarding (fractional managers can start quickly vs. lengthy full-time recruitment), scalability (fractional offers flexibility vs. fixed full-time commitment), and risk mitigation (fractional allows testing without long-term contracts). Add a subsection titled "Ease of Hiring: Fractional vs. Full-Time" with a table or bullet points for clarity.

**2. Risks of Hiring the Wrong Fractional Sales Manager Not Outlined**

* **Problem**: While challenges of hiring a sales manager are mentioned (e.g., attracting talent, failed promotions), the article doesn’t address the specific risks of hiring the wrong fractional sales manager, such as misalignment with goals or inconsistent availability.
* **Solution**: Add a section titled "Pitfalls of Hiring the Wrong Fractional Sales Manager" detailing risks like financial loss (e.g., fees for an ineffective hire), team disruption (e.g., inconsistent leadership), and strategic misalignment (e.g., lack of industry fit). Include a reflective prompt: "Imagine a fractional hire who doesn’t gel with your team—how would that hit your bottom line?"

**3. No Clear Steps for Effective Hiring**

* **Problem**: The article lacks a structured guide on how to hire a sales manager effectively, limiting its utility for readers seeking actionable advice.
* **Solution**: Introduce a section titled "Steps to Hire the Right Sales Manager" with key qualities to evaluate (e.g., proven track record, coaching experience) and a decision framework (e.g., external vs. internal hiring). Incorporate SMN’s expertise subtly: "A fractional sales manager from SMN can tick these boxes with less guesswork."

**4. Missing Reflective Prompts for Engagement**

* **Problem**: The lengthy article lacks reflective prompts to keep readers engaged, making it feel like a one-way lecture rather than an interactive resource.
* **Solution**: Weave in conversational prompts throughout, such as "How long can your business wait for results before it stings?" (in the risks section) or "Does your team need a hands-on seller or a steady coach?" (in the hiring steps). Aim for 2-3 prompts per major section to break up text and spark reader reflection.

**5. Hiring Advice Limited to SMN’s Offerings**

* **Problem**: The article’s hiring advice centers solely on SMN’s fractional services, missing broader options that could attract readers earlier in their decision-making process.
* **Solution**: Add a subsection under "Where to Find a Sales Manager" with general hiring tips (e.g., "Explore job boards like LinkedIn, tap into professional networks, or use recruiters for specialized roles"), then transition to SMN: "For a streamlined solution, SMN’s fractional sales managers cut through the noise." This balances value with promotion.

**6. Lack of References to Job Boards and Networks**

* **Problem**: Unlike recent SMN outlines and articles, this piece doesn’t mention job boards, professional networks, or other hiring channels, reducing its scope and SEO potential.
* **Solution**: In the expanded "Where to Find a Sales Manager" section, reference specific platforms (e.g., "Post on Indeed or LinkedIn to cast a wide net") and networks (e.g., "Ask your industry contacts—who’s worked wonders elsewhere?"). Link to SMN’s process as a superior alternative: "Or skip the search with SMN’s vetted experts."

**7. Underwhelming Call to Action (CTA)**

* **Problem**: The CTA ("interview us") is basic and lacks a compelling hook or urgency, failing to drive immediate action.
* **Solution**: Revamp the CTA with a reflective hook and directive: "Are sales flat? Is your team drifting? Now’s the time to act—visit salesmanagernow.com to see how a fractional sales manager delivers results fast." Place this at the end and reinforce it with mini-CTAs mid-article (e.g., "Curious? Check SMN’s approach.").

**8. Lack of Urgency Throughout the Article**

* **Problem**: The article and its final paragraph lack urgency, missing opportunities to motivate readers to act quickly on their hiring needs.
* **Solution**: Infuse urgency with phrases like "Don’t let sales stall—act now" or "The longer you wait, the more you risk losing momentum." In the final paragraph, tie urgency to SMN’s value: "Hiring the right sales manager can’t wait. SMN gets you there faster—start today at salesmanagernow.com."

**9. Poor Structure and Readability**

* **Problem**: The article isn’t well-structured, with wordy paragraphs and inadequate use of headings (H1-HX), making it hard to skim.
* **Solution**: Reorganize into clear sections with descriptive H2 headings (e.g., "Why Fractional Sales Management Works," "Steps to Hire Effectively") and an H1 title like "What Is Outsourced Sales Management—and How to Get It Right." Shorten paragraphs to 2-3 sentences, using bullets or bolded phrases (e.g., cost savings) for key points.

**10. Absence of Client Testimonials or Quantifiable Wins**

* **Problem**: The article lacks testimonials or specific data (e.g., "clients cut hiring time by X%") to back up claims, reducing credibility and engagement.
