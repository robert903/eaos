#### **Pitfalls of Coaching Sales Management Wrong**

* **Generic Coaching Plans**Ripple effect: one-size-fits-all flops when teams, markets, and styles differ—managers disengage fast.
  + **Prompt:** "Picture your manager with a plan that doesn’t fit—would they buy in or tune out?"
  + **Nudge:** Question the cookie-cutter fix—unique challenges need tailored moves.
* **Failing to Listen**
  Risk: skipping a manager’s real struggles—like morale or resources—keeps issues buried.
  + **Reflective Twist:** "If coaching talks over them, how can it fix what matters?"
  + **Nudge:** Dig into their world—unheard challenges don’t solve themselves.
* **Overemphasis on Team Metrics**
  Contrast: obsessing over quotas hides leadership gaps, missing how managers motivate or strategize.
  + **Mental Exercise:** "What’s slipping through when you only see numbers—morale, vision?"
  + **Nudge:** Look beyond the spreadsheet—metrics alone mask the real story.
* **Setting Unrealistic Leadership Goals**
  Downside: demanding overnight wins or advanced skills without support erodes confidence.
  + **Prompt:** "Imagine your sales manager crushed by impossible targets—would they rise or retreat?"
  + **Nudge:** Set them up to succeed—pressure without a foundation backfires.

* **Understand Their Needs**
  Assess gaps: leadership confidence, team motivation, strategy execution.

* **Build a Coaching Plan**
  Tailor it: start with a template, adjust for their world.

  + **Track Wins:** "Sales up, morale steady—proof it’s working."
  + **Tie to SMN:** "SMN’s plans cut the chaos—customized, not canned."
* **Motivate & Retain Talent**
  Teach techniques: small wins, accountability, real talk.

  + **Enforce Standards:** "Do they know the bar—and how to raise it?"
