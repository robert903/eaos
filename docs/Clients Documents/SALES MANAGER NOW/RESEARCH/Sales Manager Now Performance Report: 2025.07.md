### **Overall Organic Performance**

* **Total Impressions:** Declined from 91,400 to 84,100 (-8.0%).

* The primary challenge for paid search this month is a critical **attribution issue** that masks a continued lack of verifiable conversions. The core challenge remains a severe disconnect in the post-click experience, where the promise of the ad is not being met by the landing page, costing $525.28 with no return.

#### **Note on Attribution**

* The single conversion reported by Google Ads came from the **"[BM, L] Competitors 1"** campaign, *a campaign designed to target the audience of SMN's direct competitors*. However, analysis of the lead data confirms this conversion was from Tom Pittman, whose meeting was attributed to the "app.agolix.com / referral" source and was a personal request to reconnect.
* This indicates a technical misattribution, where the Google Ads tracking pixel likely fired for a user who had previously interacted with an ad but whose motivation and final conversion path were unrelated to paid search. For our analysis, we will consider paid search to have generated **0 attributable conversions** in July.

## **Key Takeaways**

* **#1 Problem: Missed Opportunity on Core Terms:** The primary issue has evolved. The most critical challenge is now the failure to convert soaring impression counts on core commercial pages and keywords into clicks.
* **Paid Search Funnel Remains Broken:** The single conversion reported by Google Ads was confirmed to be a misattribution, meaning the entire **$525.28 ad spend was ineffective** and generated zero attributable leads. This highlights a critical failure in the post-click funnel, pointing to an urgent issue with landing page performance or audience-to-offer match.
* **Organic Search Remains the Value Driver:** Despite a drop in overall traffic, organic search was the only channel to deliver multiple, high-quality leads in July, justifying a focused effort to resolve the organic CTR and traffic issues.
* **Content Visibility is Not Translating to Traffic:** Specific pages are being rewarded by Google with thousands of additional impressions. This is a clear signal of authority, but the lack of corresponding click growth represents the single largest opportunity for immediate traffic recovery.

## **Next Steps**

* **Priority #1: Immediately Capitalize on High-Impression, Zero-Click Pages (Organic)**Our most significant opportunity is to fix the glaring disconnect on our highest-potential pages. This is the fastest path to a substantial increase in relevant traffic.
  + **Action:** Rewrite the meta titles and descriptions for the /what-is-outsourced-sales-management/ and /services/sales-coaching/ pages. The new snippets must be benefit-driven, directly address user problems, and create a compelling reason to click.
* **Priority #2: Diagnose the Site-Wide Impression Drop**While we focus on CTR, we must also understand the root cause of the 8% decline in overall visibility to prevent further erosion.
  + **Action:** Conduct an analysis to determine which sections of the site, keyword categories, or page types lost the most impressions. Investigate whether this was caused by seasonality, new competitor activity, or algorithm updates to inform our broader content and SEO strategy.
* **Priority #3: Build a Content Moat Around "Fractional Sales Manager"**Google is signaling that it wants to show our site for "fractional sales manager." We need to reinforce this authority.
  + **Action:** Leverage the massive impression data for "fractional sales manager" to guide new content creation. Develop targeted blog posts, downloadable resources, or case studies around this topic to capture a wider audience and provide more entry points to the site for this high-value search term.
