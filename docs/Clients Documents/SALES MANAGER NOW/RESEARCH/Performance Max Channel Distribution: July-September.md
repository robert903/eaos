### **Performance Max Campaign Analysis: Search and YouTube Dominate Spending and Engagement**

A recent Performance Max campaign allocated the vast majority of its $307.68 budget to Search and YouTube, which together accounted for over 97% of the total cost and nearly all user interactions. While the campaign generated significant impressions and clicks, it ultimately yielded zero conversions across all channels.

![](data:image/png;base64...)

**Key Findings:**

* **Budget Allocation:** The campaign heavily favored Search, which received the largest share of the budget at $162.46 (52.80%). YouTube followed closely with an expenditure of $138.80 (45.11%). In contrast, Display, Discover, Gmail, and Maps received minimal or no funding.
* **Impression and Interaction Distribution:** YouTube was the primary driver of impressions, garnering 23,761 of the total 34,832. However, the majority of interactions, which include clicks and engagements, were also concentrated on this channel, totaling 11,640. The Search channel, despite a lower number of impressions at 2,614, was efficient in generating 294 clicks.
* **Channel Performance Breakdown:**
  + **Search:** With the highest expenditure, Search proved effective at capturing user intent, securing 294 clicks from 2,614 impressions.
  + **YouTube:** This channel was instrumental in achieving broad visibility, leading in both impressions and overall interactions. It accumulated 172 clicks and a significant 11,640 interactions.
  + **Display:** A small investment of $5.94 resulted in 8,411 impressions and 42 clicks.
  + **Discover:** A minimal spend of $0.48 on this channel produced 46 impressions and a single click.
  + **Gmail and Maps:** These channels received no budget and consequently generated no activity.
