### **Overall Organic Performance**

* **Total Impressions:** Grew slightly from 90,800 to **91,400** (+0.7%).
* **Total Clicks:** Declined significantly by 43 clicks (-17.8%), from 241 to **198.**
* **Average Position:** Improved slightly from 40.9 to **40.4** (a decrease of 0.5, lower is better)

The central theme for June's performance is a declining click-through rate (CTR), which fell from 0.3% to 0.2%. While the website's overall visibility in search results remains stable and rankings have even improved, the significant drop in clicks indicates a growing disconnect between the site's search snippets (titles and descriptions) and user intent. This trend suggests that while the content is being surfaced by Google, it is failing to compel users to click at the same rate as the previous period, representing the most critical challenge to address.

## **Key Takeaways**

* **Worsening CTR is the #1 Problem:** The primary issue is no longer just about optimizing for more clicks; it's about stopping the bleed. A site-wide drop in CTR from 0.3% to 0.2% is the direct cause of the 18% decline in organic traffic, despite stable visibility.
* **Paid Search Funnel:** The Google Ads campaigns are effective at generating clicks (13.21% overall CTR) but are completely failing to convert. The entire $158.73 ad spend was ineffective, pointing to an issue with landing pages or audience-to-offer match.
* **Organic Search is the Sole Conversion Driver:** Despite lower traffic, organic search was the only channel to deliver qualified leads in June, proving its immense value and justifying a focused effort to fix the top-of-funnel CTR problem.
* **Content Presents Clear Opportunities:** Specific pages, like the "sales-management-process" page with over 1,200 impressions and zero clicks, offer a tangible, high-impact opportunity for immediate optimization and traffic recovery.

## **Next Steps**

**1. Priority #1: Convert Existing Visibility into Traffic (Organic)**

Our most significant opportunity lies in optimizing the core pages that are already earning thousands of impressions. This is the lowest-hanging fruit for generating a substantial increase in relevant traffic.

* **Action:** We will craft benefit-driven, compelling snippets that speak directly to the user's problem and clearly state what they will find on the page. The goal is to turn these high-impression, zero-click pages into valuable traffic sources.

**2. Resolve the Paid Search Conversion Gap**

While our Google Ads are successfully capturing attention (high CTRs), they are not driving business outcomes. We need to bridge the gap between the ad click and the conversion.

* **Landing Page & Ad Copy Alignment:** Conduct a rigorous audit of the post-click experience for the top-performing ad campaigns like "Fractional Sales Management."
* **Action:** Ensure the messaging, value proposition, and call-to-action on the landing page are in perfect alignment with the ad that brought the user there.

**3. Strengthen Brand & Foundational Page Engagement**

Beyond the highest-priority pages, we need to ensure the rest of the site's foundational content is performing optimally.

* **Review Brand SERP:** Analyze the search results page for "sales manager now" to understand why clicks have declined. Look for new competitors or Google features that may be distracting users, and adjust the homepage's meta description to stand out.
* **Optimize the /services/ Page:** While this page gained a click, its CTR is still extremely low relative to its 5,395 impressions. This core page needs a revised meta title and description that better summarizes the value and range of services offered.

**4. Build a Content Strategy Guided by Data**

The impression data gives us a clear roadmap for what topics Google considers the site to have authority on. We can use this to guide future efforts.

* **Action:** Leverage keywords from high-impression pages (like those related to "sales coaching" and "outsourced sales management") to create new, targeted blog posts or resources. This will help capture an even wider audience and build on the authority Google already recognizes.
