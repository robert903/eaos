# **Visitor Interaction Report: User Scroll Analysis**

This report provides an analysis of user scrolling patterns on the Sales Manager Now homepage, based on the provided heatmap and visitor data. The goal is to understand how visitors interact with the page content from top to bottom.

## **Key Observations:**

* **Strong Initial Engagement:** Nearly all visitors (99.3%) view the top of the page, indicating the initial headline, "In Need of A Sales Manager Now?", successfully captures attention.
* **Major Drop-off After Logos:** A significant number of users leave after viewing the client logo section. While 81% of users scroll 20% down the page, only 58.8% make it to the 30% mark.
* **Pricing is a Key Decision Point:** The "Your Cost" section retains significant interest. Over half of the initial visitors (51.6%) scroll down to view the pricing tiers.
* **Highly Motivated Visitors Reach the Bottom:** Only a small, but likely highly interested, group of visitors reaches the content at the bottom of the page. Approximately one-third (33%) see the details of the "Inclusive Services," and only one-in-five visitors (20.7%) scroll to the very bottom to see the downloadable guides.

## **Detailed Scroll Analysis**

The user journey down the page can be broken into four distinct phases:

**1. The Hook (0-25% Scroll Depth)**The top of the page is highly effective. As seen in the red and yellow areas of the heatmap, nearly everyone sees the initial value proposition. Engagement remains very high through the introductory text and the beginning of the client logos, with 94.7% of visitors scrolling 15% down the page.

**2. The Proof & First Drop-Off (25-40% Scroll Depth)**This section, dominated by the grid of client logos, is the page's first major drop-off point. The number of visitors falls from 63.2% at the quarter-page mark to 54.4% by the time they are 40% down the page. This suggests that while social proof is valuable, users may either be satisfied by a quick scan of the logos and scroll quickly past them, or they lose interest and leave the page here.

**3. The Consideration Phase (40-75% Scroll Depth)**This is a critical section for decision-making. Approximately half of the original visitors (51.6%) scroll to the middle of the page to evaluate the "Your Cost" and pricing tiers. The scroll rate here is more stable, indicating that users who are interested in the price are also likely to investigate the "Inclusive Services" that follow. However, engagement continues to decline, with only 39.9% of visitors reaching the 75% scroll point.

**4. The Final Conversion Opportunity (75-100% Scroll Depth)**Only the most engaged users make it to the bottom of the page. About one-third of visitors (36.4%) see the lower half of the service details. The downloadable assets at the very bottom—the Sales Management Guide, Compensation Builder, and Sales Environment Assessment—are seen by only 20.7% of the total audience. These users represent a highly qualified lead segment who are actively seeking more detailed information.

## **Conclusion & Recommendations**

The data reveals a clear path of user consideration. The initial pitch is strong, but there is a significant hurdle after the client logos. The pricing section effectively holds the attention of about half the audience.

Based on this analysis, you could consider the following:

* **Test a more compact client logo section** to see if it reduces the user drop-off at the 25-40% scroll depth.
* **Consider moving a high-value call-to-action**, such as the "Sales Environment Assessment," higher up the page to capture users before they drop off. This could engage more users who are interested but may not have the patience to scroll to the bottom.

# **Visitor Interaction Report: User Click Analysis**

This report analyzes user click patterns on the homepage to understand visitor engagement, points of interest, and areas of potential confusion. The findings are based on a dataset of 580 total clicks from desktop users.

#### **Executive Summary**

Visitors are highly focused on understanding the cost and mechanics of your service. The **"Our Fees"** link is the most-clicked interactive element, indicating price is a primary consideration. Users are actively navigating to learn more but also show significant confusion by clicking on headings and images they expect to be interactive links. The main "Book a Meeting" call-to-action (CTA) is being used, but not as frequently as the information-seeking navigation links.

## **Key Findings from Click Data**

**1. Navigation Clicks: Visitors are Price- and Process-Oriented**

The main navigation menu is the primary tool visitors use to find information. The click distribution clearly shows their priorities:

* **Our Fees (38 clicks):** This was the single most-clicked link on the page. Visitors want to know the cost of your services above all else.
* **How We Work (19 clicks):** The second most popular link. After price, users want to understand your process and what they get for their money.
* **Contact Us (11 clicks):** A strong signal of intent from visitors who are ready to take the next step.
* **Sales Manager Now Logo (14 clicks):** Standard user behavior, clicking the logo to return to the top of the homepage.
* **Other Menu Items:** Other links like "Services" and "Resources" also received a healthy number of clicks, showing broad exploration.

**2. Call-to-Action (CTA) Engagement**

The primary "Book a Meeting" buttons are receiving attention, confirming they are visible and understood.

* The **"Book a Meeting to Learn More"** button in the main hero section received 9 clicks.
* Other identical buttons further down the page also received clicks, showing consistent user interest in this next step.

**3. Areas of User Confusion (High-Value Clicks on Non-Interactive Elements)**

A significant number of clicks were on elements that are not currently links. This indicates a strong user expectation and a desire for more information.

* **Hero Subheading (19 clicks):** The text "Staffing fractional sales managers into small businesses... More Experience | Less Cost | Better Results" received a high number of clicks. Users are particularly drawn to the underlined portion, expecting it to lead to more details.
* **Pricing Tiers Graphic (3 clicks on the image):** Visitors are clicking directly on the image of the pricing tiers. They likely expect to be taken to the "Our Fees" page or see more details about each plan.
* **Inclusive Services Diagram (2 clicks on the image):** Similarly, users are clicking on the diagram illustrating your services, expecting it to link to the "Services" page for a deeper dive.

**4. General Page Interaction**

* **Chat Widget (34 clicks):** A large number of clicks were recorded on the "minimize" button of the Zoho live chat widget. This shows the widget is being noticed, but users are actively choosing to hide it, perhaps finding it intrusive or having finished their interaction.
* **Background Clicks (127+ clicks):** Clicks on the general page background (html) and main content area (#main) are common. They often represent users trying to scroll, select text, or "rage clicking" when an element doesn't behave as expected.

## **Analysis & Recommendations**

1. **Price is the Gateway:** With "Our Fees" being the top destination, it's clear that your pricing is a critical decision point. Users are qualifying themselves based on cost early in their journey. This is a positive sign of transparent positioning.
2. **Unmet Expectations are Causing Friction:** The clicks on non-interactive headlines and images are a major finding. Users are telling you exactly what they want to know more about. This is a missed opportunity to guide them further down the funnel.
   * **Recommendation:** Make the headline text **"More Experience | Less Cost | Better Results"** a clickable link. This link should scroll the user down to the section of the page that explains these value propositions.
   * **Recommendation:** Make the entire **Pricing Tiers graphic** a single, large clickable link that directs to the "Our Fees" page.
   * **Recommendation:** Link the **"Inclusive Services" diagram** directly to your main "Services" page.
3. **Strengthen the Path to Conversion:** While users are finding the "Contact Us" page and "Book a Meeting" CTAs, the path could be clearer. The journey from information-gathering ("Our Fees," "How We Work") to action ("Book a Meeting") should be as seamless as possible.
   * **Recommendation:** On the "Our Fees" and "How We Work" pages (and any other high-traffic informational pages), ensure that a clear "Book a Meeting to Learn More" CTA is prominently featured. This captures their interest at the peak of their engagement.
